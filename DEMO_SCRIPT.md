# PublicPath — 3-Minute SNVC Demo Script

**Format:** Live product walkthrough
**URL:** https://tahvia127.github.io/PublicPath/
**Time:** ~3 minutes (180 seconds). Suggested pacing in [brackets].

## [0:00–0:25] The Hook — Set the Stage

> "There are over 20 million public sector jobs in the United States.
> That's 14.5% of the entire U.S. workforce — state and local governments,
> federal agencies, nonprofits doing public work.
>
> But if you're a student trying to break into government right now,
> there is no single place to find these jobs. You have to check USAJobs
> for federal roles, then each state's portal, then the city's portal,
> then fifteen different fellowship sites — and even then you don't know
> how to apply, how long it takes, or whether you're actually qualified.
>
> That's the problem PublicPath solves."

**[Navigate to index.html as you speak — let the landing page load]**

## [0:25–0:50] The Product — Landing Page

> "PublicPath is a centralized platform that aggregates federal, state,
> and local government jobs into one searchable interface — and delivers
> the best opportunities directly to subscribers every week.
>
> We're in active pilot right now. Our database has [gesture to live count stat]
> active listings pulled daily from seven sources: USAJobs, Jooble, Adzuna,
> and four others — all automatically refreshed every morning."

**[Point to the live job count stat in the stats bar]**

> "Subscribers sign up here, tell us their government level preference —
> federal, state, local, fellowship — and their function area. That lets us
> personalize the weekly digest to what each person actually wants."

## [1:40–2:10] The Differentiator — Resources + Weekly Digest

**[Click Resources in nav]**

> "But job listings alone aren't enough. PublicPath also demystifies
> the process. Federal hiring is confusing — the resume format is different,
> the timeline is months long, clearances are opaque.
>
> We have a full guidance layer: how to write a federal resume, what the
> hiring timeline actually looks like week by week, clearance levels explained,
> the GS pay scale, and special hiring paths for students and recent grads."

**[Scroll quickly through the Resources page]**

> "And every week, subscribers get a curated digest — hand-picked roles
> matched to their preferences, plus opportunities shared directly by
> people working inside government. That's our network picks feature —
> insider sourced roles that never show up on a job board."

## Backup / Q&A Talking Points

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| How do you make money? | B2B: sell to universities and career services offices. Better placement outcomes is a measurable ROI for them. Premium student services (resume reviews, advising) as a secondary stream. |
| Who are your competitors? | USAJobs (federal only, terrible UX), state job boards (fragmented), fellowship aggregators (limited scope). No one does cross-level aggregation with a guidance layer for early-career users. |
| Can you scale the data? | Already pulling from 7 APIs automatically. Daily sync via GitHub Actions. The pipeline is built to add sources in hours. |
| What's the roadmap? | AI-based role matching (profile → job fit score), application tracking dashboard, LinkedIn integration, university partnership program. |
| What do you need? | Pilot feedback, university partnerships, and capital to build the AI matching layer and scale nationally. |
